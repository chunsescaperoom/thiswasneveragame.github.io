This Was Never a Game

An experience that cannot be played — only preserved.

This is the closest you will ever get.

It doesn’t start like this.

You were just going to meet a friend.

You talk. You sit. Someone knocks.

A stranger falls into the room.

You keep talking.

Nothing feels wrong.

Until something explodes.

And suddenly, everything changes.

And then it makes sense.

The sky turns dark.

Things begin to fall.

Someone asks you:

“Where is your weapon?”

You don’t understand.

But you start acting like you do.

You contact a base.

You follow instructions.

You solve things to survive.

You begin to believe:

This is a war.

But something is wrong.

A child’s voice appears where it shouldn’t.

A room filled with symbols, blood, and fragments.

Something feels… personal.

Not external.

Not real.

And then, everything collapses.

Lights flash.

Voices surround you.

Like a press conference.

Like you are being watched.

And then a voice asks:

“Do you think you are a murderer?”

And everything goes dark.

It was never a battlefield.

You were on your way to meet a friend.

Something fell from the sky.

And your mind did the rest.

You didn’t enter a war.

You returned to one.

A place where you were once called a hero.

A place you never truly left.

Not all wars end when they’re over.

This experience exists in Taiwan.

It cannot be downloaded.

It cannot be streamed.

And for most people outside Taiwan, it will never be possible to experience it.

And now, it may disappear.

Not because it failed.

But because works like this are hard to sustain.

It has been described as powerful.

Unforgettable.

Even meaningful.

But not enough people are able to reach it.

So this page is not about selling a game.

It is about keeping something from being lost.
