Before you continue

This page is not intended for future players. It contains major spoilers, including visual details, thematic framing, and narrative intention. It was created for international viewers, supporters, and anyone who will never be able to access the original physical experience.

What this is

This is not a conventional escape room pitch, and it is not a digital game.

It is an archive page for a physical immersive work: a carefully structured 70-minute experience designed to simulate the disorientation, emotional fragmentation, and delayed realization of PTSD from the inside.

Participants do not enter with the knowledge that they are exploring trauma. They discover it only at the end.

Why this page exists

Most people outside Taiwan will never be able to play this work.

That distance created a problem: if the experience cannot travel, and the audience cannot reach it, then the work risks disappearing into obscurity.

This page exists to prevent that disappearance — to document the intention, preserve the atmosphere, and invite support for a work that many can only witness from afar.

How it begins

At first, the audience believes they are inside a war scenario. The space, the tension, the mission, and the emotional pressure all suggest external danger.

They respond as if survival is the objective.

What it becomes

Only after the experience is over does the final reveal reframe everything: they were not moving through war.

They were moving through the subjective reality of a PTSD patient — through memory distortion, emotional residue, and a mind trapped in unresolved trauma.

Why support it

Because some works matter even when they cannot scale. Because not every meaningful experience can become a product. Because physical storytelling can be fragile, local, and still worth preserving.
